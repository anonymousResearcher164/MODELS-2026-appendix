The behavior tree describes pasture navigation and sheltering behavior for an animal across day and night cycles. 
The animal will execute a series of actions in order, and it will select the first branch that succeeds after 
checking branches from left to right and retrying from the beginning when re-evaluated. The animal will first 
try a sequence that remembers progress and will require being on pasture, being in daytime, and being inside 
the shed before proceeding. The animal will leave the shed using custom action LeaveShed if the preceding 
conditions are true. The animal will select a random pasture field using custom action FindRandomPastureField 
after leaving the shed. The animal will move to the selected field using custom action Move to complete this branch. 
The animal will otherwise try a second memory-based sequence that will require being on pasture, being in nighttime, 
and not being inside the shed before proceeding. The animal will confirm that not being inside the shed is true by 
inverting the result of condition IsInShed. The animal will find the shed using custom action FindShed when those 
conditions hold. The animal will move toward the shed using custom action Move after locating it. The animal will 
enter the shed using custom action EnterShed to complete this branch. The animal will otherwise try a third 
memory-based sequence that will require being in daytime and not being inside the shed before proceeding. The 
animal will confirm that not being inside the shed is true by inverting the result of condition IsInShed. The animal 
will perform a random movement on pasture using custom action RandomMove when daytime holds and it is outside the shed. 
The animal will stop evaluation after the first branch succeeds, and it will re-evaluate from the beginning whenever 
the active branch is running.
